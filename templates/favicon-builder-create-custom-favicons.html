What Is a Favicon?

Favicon stands for "favorite icon." It is a little graphical picture like a logo that is shown in your program tabs with the site name in bookmarks, and it is likewise noticeable on cell phones and tablets. For the most part, It's a 16X16 and 32X32 pixel picture that can be saved exclusively in ICO and PNG formats depending on the device or browser type. Regardless of size, it also facilitates a visual identifier and strengthens a website's brand recognition and trustworthiness.

Why Are Favicons Essential?

  • Branding: A favicon helps to build a strong identity for your website that makes the site effortlessly recognizable.
  • User Experience: It improves the navigation of your website by enabling users to spot your website quickly among other open tabs of the browser.
  • Professionalism: A favicon can make your website look super smooth, professional, and trustworthy as compared to those who don't contain one.

How to Create a Favicon for a Website

Creating a favicon is not rocket science. You can easily make a professional favicon by just following the simple steps listed below:

  1. Start with a Clear Image: Select a simple graphic that visualizes your brand. Make sure to pick a simple design that stands out well in square dimensions and matches your website's color scheme. A complex design can ruin the originality and make it messy.
  2. Select a Favicon Builder Tool: Users can use free or paid versions of favicon builder tools to generate a professional favicon; even newbies can easily use free tools because of their simplicity, such as IMG2IMG, Canva, GIMP, etc.
  3. Convert to ICO Format: ICO is the ideal format for most search engines. If your logo is not in that specific format, you can easily convert it into ICO by using PNG to ICO or Image to ICO converter tools like IMGTOIMG, favicon.io, or RealFaviconGenerator and IMG2IMG.
  4. Add to Your Website: Place the favicon in your site's root directory and reference it in the HTML header: 
    <link rel="icon" type="image/x-icon" href="/favicon.ico">

Best Practices for Favicon Design

A convenient design favicon can maintain the professionalism and usefulness of your website. Check out the following steps to create an effective favicon.

  • Keep it Simple: Favicons are tiny in size, so make sure to add bold text, identical shapes, or symbols and avoid complex details. Keep it easy and catchy.
  • Use Contrasting Colors: Select high contrast that is actively visible in the tiny logo.
  • Stick to Your Brand: Keep your favicon attached to your website branding, choose an identical part of your logo, and use it in branding your website.
  • Optimize File Size: Your Favicons should be under 1KB, and an increase in size can affect the icon's loading time.
  • Check on Multiple Devices: Test the appearance of your favicon in different search engines, tabs, and operating systems to ensure evenness and quality.

How to Convert PNG to ICO Favicon

  1. Prepare Your PNG: Check if the shape of your picture is square; it should include 16x16, 32x32, or 64x64 pixels so it can be enhanced for small sizes.
  2. Use a Converter Tool: Upload your prepared PNG file using a convertor tool like IMG2IMG, ConvertICO, or Favicon.io.
  3. Download the ICO: The converter tool will create an ICO format file that will be ready to be recognized on your website.

Favicon File Formats for Websites

  • ICO: ICO stands for "Windows Icon"; it is the only format that supports various resolutions in a single file. It is the standard format for search engines to recognize the file as a favicon.

  • SVG: SVG stands for "Scalable Vector Graphics" and is ideal for high-resolution displays, but it may not be supported by all browsers.
  • GIF: GIF stands for "Graphics Interchange Format." It is not widely supported but can be used to create animated favicons.
